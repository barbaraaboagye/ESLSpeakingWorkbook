Change the direct demands into polite requests. Read them aloud to your partner.

  • 1. "Give me the menu." → "Could we have the menu, please?"

  • 3. "I want the bill." →
  • 4. "Where is the bathroom?" →
Pair Work Dietary Restrictions

Practice asking questions about the food. Student A reads the prompt. Student B asks the waiter.

  • Prompt 1: You are vegetarian (no meat).
  • Prompt 2: You are allergic to peanuts.
  • Prompt 3: You don't like spicy food.
Answers (Polite Requests): 2. Could you bring me some water, please? 3. Could we have the bill, please? 4. Could you tell me where the bathroom is?
Answers (Dietary): 1. Does this dish have meat in it? 2. I am allergic to peanuts, does this have nuts? 3. Is this food spicy?

Chapter 14

Telephone Calls

Speaking Practice I
Drill Telephone Recovery Phrases

You cannot see the other person's face. If you don't understand, you MUST stop them. Practice these out loud.

  • "I'm sorry, could you speak a little slower?"
  • "Could you repeat that, please?"
  • "I'm sorry, we have a bad connection. Can you hear me?"
Activity Leaving a Message

The person did not answer. Read your voicemail out loud.

"Hi, this is [Your Name]. I am calling about [My Car / My Appointment / The Job]. Please call me back at [Your Phone Number]. Thank you!"
Ensure students are saying their phone numbers in single digits (e.g., "Five-Five-Five" not "Five hundred fifty-five").

Drill Safe vs. Unsafe Topics

In America/Canada, some topics are "unsafe" for strangers. Tell your partner if these are SAFE or UNSAFE to ask a neighbor.

  • 1. "How much money do you make?"
  • 2. "Did you watch the sports game last night?"
  • 3. "Who did you vote for in the election?"
  • 4. "I like your shoes! Where did you get them?"
Drill The Exit Strategy

Practice interrupting a conversation politely to leave.

  • "Well, I should let you go."
  • "Look at the time! I have to run."
  • "It was so nice catching up, but I have an appointment."
1. Unsafe. 2. Safe. 3. Unsafe. 4. Safe.

Speaking Practice II
Role Play The Talkative Neighbor
Student A: The Busy Person

You are carrying heavy groceries. Your neighbor stops you. Listen for a minute, but then use an "Exit Strategy" phrase to end the conversation politely and go home.
Student B: Talkative Neighbor

You are lonely and love to talk. Talk about the weather, your cat, and the loud noise last night. Do not stop talking easily.
